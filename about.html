Project Details

Title: Data Governance and Transparency

Created by: Cody Thiess & Jordan Webster

Course: Cybertechnology Ethics - ICT 215-930C

Team: Group 5 â€“ Research Project

Assignment: Final Presentation

This website is part of a final research presentation focusing on the ethical concerns surrounding data privacy and governance in the United States. The goal of this project is to raise awareness about data practices, compare them with global standards such as the GDPR, and advocate for better transparency, consent, and accountability.

As students at the University of Wisconsin-Stout and Cybersecurity professionals, we understand the importance of data protection and governance, as well as the significant lack of public knowledge surrounding these areas. This project draws upon our academic and professional experiences and aims to address one of the most pressing cybersecurity issues in today's digital world: the need for robust data governance, privacy, and transparency.
