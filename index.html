Data Governance Awareness

In today’s digital world, many people are unaware of how their personal data is being used, shared, and sometimes exploited. Data governance and transparency are crucial to understanding how corporations handle our data—and the ethical dilemmas that arise when that data is used without clear consent. This project explores the challenges surrounding data privacy, comparing practices in the U.S. with global standards like the EU's GDPR, and raises awareness of the need for stronger regulations to protect individuals' rights.

About the Ethical Dilemma

The ethical dilemma we face today is the widespread lack of data transparency and governance in the United States. Large corporations control how user data is collected, used, and shared, often hiding their practices behind complex Terms of Service (ToS) agreements and exerting influence through lobbying efforts. This lack of transparency allows businesses to profit from user data with minimal accountability or consent, raising serious ethical concerns related to privacy, autonomy, and exploitation.

Key Issues:

  • Lack of Transparency

    Corporations often obscure their data practices behind jargon-filled contracts, leaving users unaware of how their data is being utilized or shared.

  • Political Influence and Lobbying

    Powerful tech companies actively lobby against meaningful data privacy laws, delaying or weakening efforts to implement strong regulations that could protect users.

  • Exploitation of User Data

    Businesses profit from user data without proper consent, leaving individuals with little control over how their information is used or shared.

In contrast, Europe’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) imposes strong protections, requiring transparency, accountability, and user control over personal data. This model gives individuals more control and ensures that their data is handled responsibly, which noticeably contrasts with the largely unregulated environment in the U.S.

This highlights the urgent need for further ethical discussion and policy reform in the U.S. In the absence of a comprehensive, federal-level framework, individuals are left susceptible to surveillance, manipulation, and the improper use of their data, as corporate influence continues to grow without adequate oversight.
